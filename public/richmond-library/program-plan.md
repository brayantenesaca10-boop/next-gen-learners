# Thinkers & Builders: AI Club - Richmond Library
**3-Day Series | April 8-10, 2026 | 1 hour each**

> In this hands-on, discussion-based program, students explore how to use AI as a tool for creativity, curiosity, and critical thinking. Rather than focusing on answers, participants learn how to ask better questions, understand how AI works, and use it responsibly to build projects connected to their interests (art, storytelling, problem-solving, and more). No prior AI experience is required.

## Day 2 (Thursday, April 9) - "AI vs Me & Remix Challenge"

### Opening (2 min)
- Quick recap: "Yesterday you built dream products. Today we're going to test AI and then compete with it."
- Energy check: "Who went home and tried AI on their own?" (hands up, quick shoutouts)

### Part 1: AI vs Me Challenge (20 min) - THINKERS
**Concept:** Kids and AI get the same creative prompt. They do it by hand first, then see what AI does. Compare side by side. They discover where THEY are better and where AI is better.

**Round 1 - "Describe Your Dream Room" (8 min)**
- SLIDE: Show the prompt: "Describe your dream bedroom in 3 sentences."
- Kids write it on paper (3 min, pen and paper provided)
- Then everyone types the SAME prompt into AI (2 min)
- SLIDE: Discussion question: "Which description feels more like YOU? Which one is more detailed?"
- A few kids share both versions out loud
- Key insight to draw out: AI is polished but generic. Yours has personality.

**Round 2 - "Draw a Monster" (8 min)**
- SLIDE: Show the prompt: "Create a monster that protects your school."
- Kids sketch it on paper (3 min, stick figures totally fine)
- Then everyone prompts AI to generate the same monster (2 min)
- SLIDE: Discussion question: "Which monster would you actually want protecting your school? Why?"
- Key insight to draw out: AI makes it look "better" but YOUR version has a story behind it.

**Debrief (4 min)**
- SLIDE: Two columns: "What AI does better" / "What YOU do better"
- Kids shout out answers, Brayan fills in the slide live or writes on whiteboard
- Land the message: "AI is a tool. It's powerful, but it doesn't know YOU. That's why YOU are the thinker. Now let's see what happens when the thinker uses the tool."

### Part 2: Remix Challenge (33 min) - BUILDERS
**Concept:** Everyone starts with the EXACT same prompt. Goal: take it in the wildest, funniest, most creative direction possible. Shows that the HUMAN behind the prompt is what makes AI useful.

**Setup (3 min)**
- SLIDE: Reveal the starting prompt: "A cat in a city" (or adjust based on group energy)
- SLIDE: Show the 4 award categories:
  - Most Creative
  - Funniest
  - Most Realistic
  - Most Surprising
- Rules: You can remix the prompt as many times as you want. Change words, add details, go crazy. The only rule is you START from "A cat in a city."

**Build Time (20 min)**
- Kids work on their own, remixing and generating
- Brayan walks around, hypes up good ones, helps anyone stuck
- Encourage kids to try at least 3-4 different directions before picking a favorite
- SLIDE (on screen during build time): Timer + reminder of the 4 categories

**Share & Vote (10 min)**
- Each kid shows their best creation on the big screen (or holds up their device)
- Quick 10-second explanation: "I started with a cat in a city and turned it into..."
- After all presentations, group votes for each category (hand raise or shout)
- SLIDE: Winner announcements for each category
- No losers: everyone made something different from the same starting point, which IS the lesson

### Wrap (5 min)
- SLIDE: "What did you learn today?"
  - AI is a tool, not a replacement for you
  - The person behind the prompt matters more than the prompt itself
  - Same starting point, totally different results, because YOU are different
- SLIDE: "Tomorrow is the finale. You're going to find a REAL problem in your life and build a REAL solution. Come ready to think big."

## Day 3 (Friday, April 10) - "Build Something Real"

### Part 1: Problem Finder (15 min)
- Prompt: "What's something annoying, boring, or hard in your life?"
- Each kid picks a real problem (homework, chores, a hobby challenge, something at school)

### Part 2: AI Solution Sprint (35 min)
- Use AI to brainstorm solutions to their problem
- Pick one solution and build a quick pitch:
  - Name
  - Tagline
  - One AI-generated image
  - 30-second explanation
- Option to use Lovable again for a simple landing page, or just create a "poster" with their AI image + text

### Part 3: Pitch & Vote (10 min)
- Each kid presents their idea in 30 seconds
- Group votes on favorites
- Ends on a high note - everyone leaves with something they made about something they care about

---

## Why This Flow Works
- Day 1: Explore interests + learn the tools
- Day 2: Playful, low-stakes (no "wrong" answers), builds critical thinking
- Day 3: Connects back to interests but goes deeper (real problem -> real idea)
- All three days hit "Thinkers" (critical thinking, discussion) AND "Builders" (making things)
